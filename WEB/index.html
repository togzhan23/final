Featured Authors

J.K. Rowling

Author of the Harry Potter series, a fantasy saga that has captivated readers worldwide.

Notable Books:
  • Harry Potter
  • The Casual Vacancy

George Orwell

Known for his incisive social commentary and dystopian works, including '1984' and 'Animal Farm'.

Notable Books:
  • Animal Farm
  • 1984

Jane Austen

Regarded as one of the greatest novelists in the English language, known for her keen insights into social class.

Notable Books:
  • Pride and Prejudice
  • Mansfield Park

Stephen King

Master of horror and suspense, known for his engaging storytelling and unforgettable characters.

Notable Books:
  • The Shining
  • It

Ernest Hemingway

Hemingway is famous for his economical and understated writing style, known as the "Iceberg Theory," where the deeper meaning is hidden beneath the surface. His works often explore themes of heroism, war, and human endurance.

Notable Books:
  • The Old Man and the Sea
  • A Farewell to Arms

Gabriel García Márquez

A key figure in magical realism, García Márquez blends realistic narrative with fantastical elements, exploring themes of solitude, love, and destiny in rich, evocative prose.

Notable Books:
  • One Hundred Years of Solitude
  • Love in the Time of Cholera

Toni Morrison

Morrison’s works delve into African-American experiences and history, often incorporating lyrical and layered language. Her storytelling is deep, rich with symbolism, and explores themes of identity, trauma, and community.

Notable Books:
  • Beloved
  • Song of Solomon

Fyodor Dostoevsky

A master of psychological fiction, Dostoevsky's works examine the human condition, moral dilemmas, and philosophical questions. His narratives often focus on the struggles of the poor and the nature of redemption.

Notable Books:
  • Crime and Punishment
  • The Brothers Karamazov
